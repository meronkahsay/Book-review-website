Born a Crime: Stories from a South African Childhood" is a memoir by South African comedian and former host of "The Daily Show," Trevor Noah. The book chronicles Noah's upbringing in apartheid South Africa, focusing on the mixed race identity he was born into and the challenges he faced while navigating a racially divided society. It also explores the strong bond he had with his mother, Patience, and her role in shaping his life.

Photo Source:Pinterest

The Power of Habit," a 2012 book by Charles Duhigg, explores how habits are formed and how they can be changed. The book argues that understanding the "habit loop" of cue, routine, and reward can help individuals, organizations, and even societies improve their lives. Duhigg's work has become a popular resource for self-improvement and organizational change, drawing on scientific research and real-world examples to illustrate his theories.
