Original title: Born a Crime

Author: Trevor Noah

‘Abel wanted a traditional marriage with a traditional wife…but he never fell in love with subservient women…’ He only wants a woman who is free because his dream is to put her in a cage.’

Patricia greatly challenged the traditional gender roles that dictated what women should and should not do. She raised her son independently for the better part of his life. The story highlights the strength and resilience of South African women, especially Trevor's mother, who defied traditional expectations by providing for her family despite facing adversity from all aspects of life.


Most modern men love independent women because of the packs they come with. They love the feel of not having to provide everything in the house themselves, having someone to depend on whenever things are not working for them, and sharing bills equally. Despite women also working the same hours as their men, they’re still expected to arrive home early, take care of all household chores, look after the children, and still share the bills, while men sit around after work and expect their women to serve them.

This is so relatable to Patricia’s own experience, where she worked so hard every day to provide for her children despite society trying to push her down. She never let Abel’s desire for control and power win. She still supportetd him in his garage business while still providing for her family, but Abel still craved control over her life. He wanted to dictate where and when she goes, and even Trevor’s relationship with his father.

When it comes to relationships, most men find themselves in love with independent women until they get married. They start feeling the urge to be superior in the name of being ‘head of the family’. This is very absurd considering they knew exactly who they were falling in love with, but still have the masculine urge to be powerful. They mostly feel threatened whenever they see their women thriving more than them, and that's when violence comes in most of the time, just for them to feel more powerful, in control, and whole.
